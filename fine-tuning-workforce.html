Before the Techniques

0 · The Real Question

Fine-tuning isn't one lever. It's a family of methods that trade parameter count, memory footprint, alignment power, and deployment complexity against each other — the question was never just "how do we improve the model," it's what level of control you need, and what cost you're willing to carry to get it.

What determines your family. Strong labeled data points toward SFT. Pairwise human preferences point toward DPO. Sparse reward signals point toward PPO/RLHF. A tight memory budget points toward the PEFT family — QLoRA specifically fits a 65B model's tuning onto a single 48GB GPU, per Dettmers et al. (2023).

Where this guide's claims come from. Every technique below traces to its original paper — LoRA, QLoRA, DPO, PPO, RLHF/InstructGPT, Prefix Tuning, Adapter Tuning, BitFit, IA³, and Prompt Tuning all have a real, checkable citation in the bibliography at the bottom.
