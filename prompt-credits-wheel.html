How to draft a prompt, from absolute zero

A prompt is just an instruction to an AI model, written in plain language. The difference between a weak one and a strong one almost always comes down to five ingredients — include them, and most of the 16 rules on the wheel become second nature.

🎭

Role

Who should the AI act as? "Act as a resume coach" narrows every answer that follows.

📌

Context

What background does it need? Audience, situation, anything that isn't obvious from the question alone.

🎯

Task

The exact action, stated as a verb: write, summarize, compare, list, critique.

🖹️

Format

How should the answer look? Length, structure, bullet points vs. prose, tone.

👀

Example

Show, don't just tell. One good example removes most of the guesswork.

❌ Vague prompt

"Help me with my resume."

→
✅ Specific prompt

"Act as a resume coach for a mid-level marketing role. Review this resume, list the 3 weakest bullet points, and rewrite each in under 20 words, focused on measurable results: [paste resume]"

That's it. Role, Context, Task, Format, Example — you don't need all five every time, but the more of them you include, the fewer follow-up messages (and credits) it takes to get what you actually wanted. Every rule on the wheel below is really just a different way of applying these five ingredients well.
