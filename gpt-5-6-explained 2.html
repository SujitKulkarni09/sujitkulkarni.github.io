Chapter 01 · The basics

GPT-5.6 isn't one model — it's three.

Instead of a single release, OpenAI shipped a family of three models built on a shared foundation but tuned differently for capability, speed, and cost — the same tiered idea Anthropic uses with Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus.

Flagship

Sol

The most capable tier — built for deep reasoning, complex coding, and research work. OpenAI positions it as its best coding model yet, and its strongest model to date for cybersecurity work.

$5 in / $30 out per million tokens
Balanced

Terra

The everyday workhorse — strong output quality without Sol's cost or latency. This is where most production use is expected to land by default.

$2.50 in / $15 out per million tokens
Lightweight

Luna

The fast, budget-friendly tier — built for high-volume, lower-stakes tasks where speed and cost matter more than maximum capability.

$1 in / $6 out per million tokens
Chapter 02 · What changed

The genuinely new pieces

Beyond raw capability gains, four things distinguish this release from GPT-5.5.

A new "max" reasoning effort

+

An additional effort setting that lets Sol reason for longer before answering, aimed at the hardest problems where accuracy matters more than speed.

"Ultra" mode with subagents

+

Rather than one model working alone, ultra mode coordinates multiple subagents in parallel to accelerate complex, multi-step work.

Large efficiency gains

+

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman told CNBC that the new model is 54% more token-efficient on agentic coding tasks than its predecessor — meaning it can do comparable work using roughly half the tokens.

ChatGPT Work

+

A new companion product built specifically for workplace tasks — drafting documents, spreadsheets, and presentations — running on desktop, web, and mobile for enterprise teams.

Chapter 05 · How it rolled out

From preview to public release

June 26, 2026

Limited preview

GPT-5.6 Sol was first previewed in restricted form, alongside early benchmark results focused on coding, biology, and cybersecurity capability.

July 9, 2026

Broad public release

OpenAI released the full GPT-5.6 family — Sol, Terra, and Luna — across ChatGPT, Codex, and the API, alongside GPT Live 1 (formerly GPT-Bidi 1). Rollout was global but gradual, reaching full availability within about 24 hours.

Ongoing

Access tightening for high-risk use

OpenAI requires hardware-backed passkeys by September 1, 2026 to retain access to its most cyber-capable frontier models, with additional restrictions for high-risk jurisdictions and entities.

Chapter 06 · Why this release is different

The cybersecurity story is the real headline

OpenAI describes GPT-5.6 as its strongest cybersecurity model yet — capable of vulnerability research, patching, and threat modeling at a level significant enough to trigger new safeguards.

High

Biological & Chemical

All three models — including the smaller Terra and Luna — were rated "High" risk in this tracked category, the first time smaller models in a family reached that designation.

High

Cybersecurity

Sol shifts the performance-efficiency frontier on long-horizon security tasks, including vulnerability research, while OpenAI states it does not cross the "Cyber Critical" threshold under its own framework.

Below High

AI Self-Improvement

The one tracked category where the family stayed under the "High" designation, per OpenAI's Preparedness Framework evaluation.

Because of this capability jump, access is layered: qualified researchers get deeper access through a program called Trusted Access for Cyber, hardware-backed passkeys become mandatory for full access by September 1, and OpenAI says it is restricting access in high-risk jurisdictions. Sam Altman has also said OpenAI worked closely with senior U.S. administration officials ahead of the deployment.

Chapter 07 · Where you'll meet it

It's already inside tools you may use at work

Microsoft 365 Copilot

OpenAI confirmed GPT-5.6 as the primary model powering Microsoft 365 Copilot — even as Microsoft simultaneously tests its own in-house "MAI" models to reduce dependence on outside providers.

Competitive positioning

Coverage of the launch frames it as squarely aimed at Anthropic, OpenAI's closest rival, following similar releases the same week from SpaceXAI and Meta — a sign of how fast the frontier-model race is currently moving.

Reference

Terms used in this guide

Sol / Terra / Luna
The three GPT-5.6 model tiers, from most to least capable — and most to least expensive.
Agentic coding
AI working semi-independently across multiple steps of a coding task — planning, writing, testing, and fixing — rather than answering a single prompt.
Terminal-Bench 2.1
A public benchmark testing how well an AI model handles command-line workflows requiring planning and tool use.
Preparedness Framework
OpenAI's internal system for tracking and mitigating the risk of severe harm from increasingly capable models.
Blue teaming
Simulating an attack on your own systems to find and fix weaknesses before real attackers do.
Subagents
Smaller AI processes coordinated by a main model to work on parts of a task in parallel, used in GPT-5.6's "ultra" mode.
ChatGPT Work
A new OpenAI product built specifically for enterprise workplace tasks like documents, spreadsheets, and presentations.
